In nuclear physics mass measurement is an essential tool for understanding the strong force and the structure of the nucleus. This is done by measuring the mass of neutron rich or proton rich isotopes approaching the nucleon dripline as these extremities are where the strong force can no longer bind nucleons (even theoretically) to the nucleus.
